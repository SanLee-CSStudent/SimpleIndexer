We believe in strong student-faculty interactions. We cultivate a communal atmosphere. These qualities are some of the many reasons to choose Lyon. Our undergraduate liberal arts college, nestled in the foothills of the Ozarks, was founded in 1872 and is one of the more historic colleges in Arkansas. We challenge our students to grow in a multi-faceted approach with a strong commitment to liberal arts education in order to mature into well-rounded, open-minded individuals. So, why Lyon? These should answer your question: Lyon has been named as a top college by Forbes, as the Most Socially Beneficial College in Arkansas, and as a Best Southeastern College by The Princeton Review. Lyon faculty teaches all classes available to students; our approach focuses on personalized, quality education, so we do not have classes taught by graduate assistants or teaching assistants. Lyon undergraduate students boasts 90 percent acceptance rates to medical and law schools. Lyon offers the Nichols International Studies Program, ensuring that students have the opportunity to explore various cultures during undergraduate years. Lyon enrolls approximately 700 students from 28 states and 13 countries, bringing a community of diverse experiences and ideas to Batesville. Want to learn more about what Lyon offers? Admission is not limited by age, race, gender, disability, sexual orientation, or national origin of the applicant. Lyon expects students to demonstrate their seriousness of purpose by participating in a college preparatory curriculum throughout their high school careers. We highly recommend that preparatory work includes the following subjects: English (4 units): to cover grammar, composition, and literature. Social Studies (3 units): to include 1 unit in American history, 1 unit in world history, and 1\/2 unit in civics or American government. Natural Sciences (3 units): to be selected from offerings in biology, chemistry, and physics. Mathematics (3 units): to include Algebra I, and additional units selected from algebra II, geometry, trigonometry, pre-calculus, and calculus. Foreign Language (2 units): to be selected from offerings in French, German, Spanish, or other major foreign languages To determine your admission, Lyon College will holistically review each file to consider success and fit. The College will offer test optional applications for prospective students. Please contact your admissions counselor for the additional test optional requirements.
