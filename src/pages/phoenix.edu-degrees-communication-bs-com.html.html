COMM\/110: Introduction to Oral Communication View course details 3 Credits This course will provide students with the basic concepts of oral presentations. HRM\/300T: Fundamentals of Human Resource Management View course details 3 Credits This course explores the critical role of human resources in achieving business results. The course will help students to have a solid understanding of the fundamentals of human resource management and its strategic relevance in business today. This course will provide students with a critical perspective on the development of human capital in the context of a unified system of attracting, retaining and developing talent that creates and supports the vision and values of the organization. Students will develop an understanding of the critical business implications for human resource professionals today. MGT\/362T: Change Management and Implementation View course details 3 Credits This course will provide students with an overview of the role that business analytics has in the preparation and implementation of change management throughout a project. Students will examine the functions of change management to apply them to activities to meet the strategic and operational plans of an organization. Upon completion of this course, students will understand the importance of the integration of data and decision making on change processes and project management methodologies to implement changes throughout an organization. Focus will also be on managing change to ensure high performance, quality, and operational effectiveness. MGT\/411: Innovative and Creative Business Thinking View course details 3 Credits This course provides students with the skills and knowledge necessary for using innovative and creative thinking strategies to improve managerial decision making and problem solving. Emphasis is placed upon learning critical skills to identify and facilitate innovative behavior and collaboration within the organization that will increase sustainable business growth and strengthen abilities to respond to organizational changes and challenges. Course lectures, reading and projects span theory and practice and draw upon examples from multiple industry sectors. MGT\/445: Organizational Negotiations View course details 3 Credits This course provides an overview of negotiations in an organizational setting. Students also examine conflict management techniques and emerging negotiation trends in globalization and technology. MGT\/498: Strategic Management View course details 3 Credits This course gives students the opportunity to integrate management concepts and practices to contemporary business strategies, while discussing the theories of strategic management. Special emphasis is placed on business ethics, sustainability, innovation, and the legal environment of business. LDR\/305T: Foundations of Leadership View course details 3 Credits This course provides an overview of leadership theories, models, and principles. LDR\/306T: Organizational Leadership: Building a Performance Culture View course details 3 Credits In this course, students will examine practices used to engage, inspire, and successfully lead people and organizations, with a focus on motivation, behavioral management, employee development and empowerment, diversity, culture, and performance improvement. LDR\/307T: Leadership Strategy and Decision Making View course details 3 Credits Effective decision making takes a variety of perspectives and information into account, using reliable sources and reasoned approaches in support of an overall strategy. This course investigates business tactics and problem-solving techniques used in long- and short-term planning, organizational governance, innovation and creative disruption, and efficiency improvement. Topics include business model analysis and design, resource allocation, quality enhancement, contingency planning, and entrepreneurship. LDR\/308T: Communication Strategies for Leaders View course details 3 Credits Timely, informative, and inclusive communication is the cornerstone of successful leadership. LDR\/309T: Leading Through Change View course details 3 Credits This course provides an overview of operational and technological considerations involved in project management and organizational change. Communications studies can help you gain verbal, written and multimedia skills that improve how you get your point across, whether that\u2019s while talking with coworkers (interpersonal communication) or as a communications specialist for your company (organizational communications). In this on-campus or online communications degree, you\u2019ll learn communication theory and have opportunities to learn hands-on with projects, papers and assignments. What can you do with a Bachelor of Science in Communications degree? As a communications major, you\u2019ll learn skills that could help you prepare to be an editor, copywriter, media relations specialist or other communications specialist. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, communication occupations are expected to grow 4% between 2019 and 2029. Is this degree accredited? University of Phoenix has been continually accredited by the Higher Learning Commission (HLC, at hlcommission.org) and its predecessor, since 1978. Can I get my communications degree online? Yes, you can choose to earn your Bachelor\u2019s of Science in Communications degree in a completely online program that\u2019s always on \u2014 24\/7\/365. As a communications student in our online program, you\u2019ll have all the support that comes with an on-campus course and greater flexibility in your schedule. Why get a Bachelor of Science in Communication from University of Phoenix? Our communications degree program is built from the ground up to help prepare you for a career in communications.
