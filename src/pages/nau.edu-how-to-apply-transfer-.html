Whether you choose to transfer to NAU\u2019s Flagstaff campus, one of our many statewide locations, or NAU Online, we\u2019ll support your smooth transition so you can focus on finishing your degree. Flagstaff campus Transfer to our main campus with majestic views of the San Francisco Peaks, a diverse and engaging campus experience, and a robust university community. Transfer now Statewide locations Complete your degree at one of our partner locations across the state\u2014including community colleges in the valley, our sister campus at NAU\u2013Yuma, and beyond. Transfer now Traditional online Finish your degree through our traditionally structured online programs, earning a high-quality university degree from wherever you learn best. Transfer now Personalized Learning online Take control of your path through our Personalized Learning programs, completing your degree on a flexible schedule that\u2019s unique to your needs. Transfer now Admission requirements for transfer students Admission You will be offered admission to NAU as a transfer student if: You have completed AGEC, AGEC-B, AGEC-S, or California IGETC with a cumulative GPA of 2.50; or you\u2019ve completed an associate degree with a cumulative GPA of 2.00. Note that with a completed AGEC or IGETC you will have fulfilled all of the lower-level requirements, though not the junior- or senior-level writing requirement, of the NAU Liberal Studies program. Without a completed AGEC or IGETC, you are still eligible to apply appropriate credits toward your NAU degree. Consideration You will be considered if you have: You have no more than one deficiency in any two areas of the required core courses. If you have a combination of a math and lab science deficiency, you are not yet admissible. and You have 12-23 transferable credits and a core high school GPA of 2.00 or above, or you have 24 or more transferable college or university credit hours with a cumulative GPA of at least 2.00.
