Hammerschmidt Lecture Series Reports and Policies Vendor Information Northark to host Cave Region Review music and arts festival by Northark Office of Public Relations Wed Apr 13, 2022 at 01:56 PM Music, arts and crafts, free food, activities for kids, and door prizes can all be found at the Cave Region\u2019s Music and Arts Festival, Saturday, April 23, 11 am-4 pm at the Pioneer Pavilion, located at North Arkansas College (Northark) south campus in Harrison. Entry into the festival is free. Artists include Shawnna Werner, Sandy Reavis, Rita Szabo, Amanda Case, Christine Ayres, Tara Young, Cindy Dollard, Dana Byrd, Blair Kimberlin, cartoonist Anthony Hunter, and many more. Visitors can interact with woodworkers, painters, photographers, jewelry makers, candle makers, and much more. There will be plenty of items to purchase. Visitors might find a nice piece of art, scented candles, or a new necklace. Additionally, local craftsman Jon Dezort will be printing several designs on shirts, and guests are encouraged to bring their own T-shirts and sweatshirts if they would like to have them printed that day. While drifting among the many artists\u2019 booths, area musicians will perform on stage. The music creates an exciting atmosphere, and there will be seats available to sit and enjoy the performers, including Ashtyn Barbaree, Aurali, Andrew Delaney, Still on the Hill, and Aaron Smith and The Coal Biters. The event is family friendly, and there are plenty of activities for kids, or kids at heart, such as face painting, beadwork, and other interactive creative projects designed by some of the artists. Let\u2019s not forget the food: Tip Top Catering is serving a sit-down meal, including pulled pork sandwiches, Calico beans, and chips. There will also be cookies, punch, water, and tea available. The latest issue of the Cave Region Review, volume 12, will also be available. The journal includes poetry, short stories, photography, and artwork by artists and writers from across the Ozarks region, as well as a few featured guests from outside the area. The festival is supported in part by the William P. & Susan Stiritz Endowed Chair in the Humanities, the North Arkansas College Foundation, North Arkansas College, and other local donors. Radio advertising is provided by the Liquidation Outlet.
