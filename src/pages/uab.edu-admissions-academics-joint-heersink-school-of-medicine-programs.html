But we're also pretty well known in other academic disciplines, too. Which is why our Joint UAB Heersink School of Medicine Programs are so popular. These programs team leading health-care experts with leading experts in other areas. Together, they can prepare you for a research or health-care career that could be, well, a big deal. Biomedical Engineering UAB Heersink School of Medicine | School of Engineering Biomedical engineers play important roles in modern health care\u2014from diagnosis and analysis to treatment and recovery. Things like implants (such as pacemakers and artificial hips), stem cells, and other technologies exist thanks to biomedical engineers. At UAB, our BME department is pretty good. After all, we rank fifth in the nation for research funding from the National Institutes of Health. So actually, very good. Major in Biomedical Engineering Bioinformatics UAB Heersink School of Medicine | College of Arts & Sciences Bioinformatics connects biological sciences, genetics, chemistry, computer science, data science, IT, engineering, applied mathematics, biostatistics, computing, and biomedical engineering. Basically, a whole lot of info is crammed into your brain so you can help treat disease and improve lives. And we\u2019re the first undergraduate bioinformatics program in Alabama. So, not too shabby. Major in Bioinformatics Cancer Biology UAB Heersink School of Medicine | College of Arts & Sciences As the only university in the U.S. with a cancer biology undergraduate program, UAB is the perfect place for you to prepare for a career in research, treatment, and other areas. The fact that we\u2019re home to a 90-ton cyclotron (one of a few in America) to zap cancer cells with precision, well, that doesn\u2019t hurt your education either. Major in Cancer Biology Genetics and Genomic Sciences UAB Heersink School of Medicine | College of Arts & Sciences Genetics and genomics make up the informational blueprint of life. Understanding that blueprint can answer a lot\u2014and we mean a lot\u2014of questions. There are only a few universities across the nation that offer a program to help you answer those questions. And we\u2019re one of them. From research to training to education, we have the tools to help you achieve your career goals. Major in GGS Immunology UAB Heersink School of Medicine | College of Arts & Sciences Immunology is the study of the structure and function of the immune system at the molecular, cellular, and physiological level. In other words, you know that army of antibodies in your body that fights off disease? Our job is to teach you what makes it tick. We cover biology, microbiology, chemistry, physics\u2014the whole gamut. No wonder U.S. News & World Report ranked us No. 27 in the \u201CBest Global Universities for Immunology\u201D poll for showing \u201Cstrength in producing research related to immunology.\u201D Major in Immunology Neuroscience UAB Heersink School of Medicine | College of Arts & Sciences It takes brains to study neuroscience. After all, it\u2019s the most rapidly advancing field of research and training out there right now. Oh, and neuroscience is literally the study of the structure and function of the nervous system with a special focus on the brain. So yeah, you kind of have to be, you know, brainy.
