Jazz vocalist Gregory Porter will perform in concert at the University of Alabama at Birmingham on Friday, June 17. Porter\u2019s performance is presented by UAB\u2019s Alys Stephens Performing Arts Center. Tickets to the 8 p.m. show are $45, $55 and $65. For tickets, call 205-975-2787 or visit AlysStephens.org. Known for his warm baritone vocals, Porter rose to acclaim in the 2010s with his earthy, cross-pollinated brand of jazz, soul and gospel. A gifted singer of standards as well as more contemporary soul material, Porter has earned favorable comparisons to his idols Nat King Cole, Donny Hathaway and Stevie Wonder, according to his artist biography. Porter earned a Grammy nod for Best Jazz Vocal Album for his 2010 debut, \u201CWater.\u201D Two years later, his second album, \u201CBe Good,\u201D further showcased Porter\u2019s growing confidence. After signing to Blue Note, he gained even more acclaim with his third album, 2013\u2019s \u201CLiquid Spirit\u201D \u2014 which hit No. 2 on the jazz charts and won the Grammy for Best Jazz Vocal Album. It also became one of the most streamed jazz albums of all time, with more than 20 million streams. \u201CTake Me to the Alley,\u201D released in early 2016, featured Porter\u2019s own version of \u201CHolding On,\u201D a track he co-wrote and previously recorded with electronic act Disclosure.
