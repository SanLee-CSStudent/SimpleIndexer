Student Involvement View More Student Life The office of Student Life generates a lively and compelling campus life which contributes to each student's learning and growth. Lyon College is committed to promoting the health and well-being of the college community by providing quality healthcare and educational outreach programs. Student Life Beyond Career Services The Career Center prepares students for life after Lyon and continues to support them as alumni. Services start during a student's college career. The Career Center exposes students to internship opportunities, prepares them to compete for the opportunities, and helps them succeed beyond college. visit the career center Health and Wellness The Office of Health and Wellness is committed to promoting the health and well-being of the Lyon College community by providing quality healthcare and educational outreach programs. visit the health & wellness center The Counseling Center A full-time licensed professional counselor provides services in a safe, supportive, and confidential environment at no cost to the student. Numerous books, pamphlets, and local community resources are available through referral by the counselor. visit the counseling center Housing and Dining Lyon's housing and dining systems emphasize the tight-knight campus community. Edwards Commons, Lyon's 44,000-square-foot campus center, is the community hub, housing a game room, a cardio room, and the Patterson Dining Hall, which serves breakfast, lunch, and dinner options every day. Housing and Dining Residential Life On-campus residential life is designed around small group living, with a live-in faculty member, student resident directors, and resident assistants supervising each residence. The live-in faculty member designs programs to integrate the academic and co-curricular aspects of campus life. Off-Campus Living Students who are veterans, reside with their spouse, or are 21 years of age or older may reside off-campus if they given written notice to the director of campus life before the assignment of on-campus housing. Lyon-owned or non-college apartments and houses are available in the Batesville community. Dining Services Lyon's dining system provides a range of options and hours, as well as flexible meal plans for students living on- and off-campus. Students can eat in the Patterson Dining Hall, the Lyon Den, or the Salty Dog Coffee Shop. Meals include fresh fruits and salads, home-style entr?es, freshly baked pizza and pasta, vegetarian options, Brenda's freshly baked desserts, and much more. Batesville Restaurants Batesville has an excellent restaurant scene, particularly downtown in the town's epicenter. Click here and treat yourself to a delicious local hotspot. Many local businesses offer Lyon League discounts. Pet Life Bring a bit of home with you to Lyon College Lyon College is the only pet-friendly college in Arkansas, meaning the College allows students to bring their furry friends to school. With plenty of open space for dog-walking and many sunny windows for cat-napping, Lyon's campus can serve as a great home for your best friend. For more information, check out our Pet FAQs. Pet Life Pet Life at Lyon It's a walk in the park! But before you move your pet's bed to your new room, there are a few guidelines you need to know: Currently, Lyon allows only full-grown dogs and cats under fifty pounds to live in the pet-friendly residence hall To live in the pet-friendly residence hall, you must register your cat or dog with the Director of Residence Life All pets must meet the registration and inoculation requirements Dogs or cats are only allowed in pet-friendly housing with proper approval Fish and other aquatic animals are allowed in all residence halls Emotional Support Animals Lyon College's policies on emotional support animals (ESA) function separately from its pet-friendly housing. A student approved for an emotional support animal may live in any residence without paying extra fees for their pet. Outdoor Recreation Nestled in the eastern foothills of the Ozark Mountains and near the beautiful White River, Lyon takes advantage of its incredible location with the Lyon Education and Adventure Program (LEAP), which provides students with access to outdoor activities in the surrounding area. Activities on campus include a bouldering wall, disc golf course, ropes course, and bluff-side hiking and biking trails. Students can take avantage of hiking, biking, paddling, camping, caving, rock climbing, fishing, and hunting options in the surrounding area. take the leap On-Campus Activities The Campus is equipped with a bouldering wall, disc golf course, two hiking and biking trails totaling approximately 5 miles, and a ropes course including both high and low elements.
