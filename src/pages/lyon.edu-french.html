You can learn more than a new language at Lyon College. You will gain critical thinking and problem-solving skills, and learn about a variety of French-speaking cultures. Through literary analysis and studying abroad, you can connect your personal passions with the larger world. Lyon prepares French majors and minors for graduate school, teaching, and a range of professions, both in the U.S. and abroad. % of French majors are employed or in grad school within 6 months of graduation :1 student-to-teacher ratio Meet the Faculty Details Courses Careers Admissions Internships Scholarships Why study French at Lyon College? Lyon\u2019s close community is perfect for learning another language. Small class sizes, student-faculty mentorships, and weekly conversation sessions help students improve their oral and written French skills. Lyon also has an exchange program at the Centre de Francais Langue Etrangere in Poitiers, France. Students can study in France for a semester or a year while taking courses with the Universit? de Poitiers. Faculty Members Dr. James Martell, Dr. Awah Mfossi Sidjeck Ways To Get Involved Learning French doesn\u2019t just happen in the classroom. The French Club offers many activities for students. These include trips to New Orleans and the Memphis opera and events like Crepe Days and masquerades. The Lyon Film Cycle shows multiple European films each semester. Presentations and discussions follow the film. You can also attend the weekly Pause Caf?, where students discuss cultural and everyday events. Major Requirements Students need a minimum of 31 credits beyond the 100-level to finish the major. As many as 6 of these credits can be taken during a summer abroad in France or a French-speaking country and as many as 15 credits can be taken during a semester abroad. View a full listing of French courses. Careers for French Majors and Minors Careers for French majors and minors include French language instructors, translators, and interpreters, as well as a variety of careers in French and Francophone speaking countries and regions.
