More Info Admissions We can help! Whether you plan to enroll in college for the first time, transfer to Northwest-Shoals Community College (NW-SCC) from another institution, return to school after time off, or are a current NW-SCC student, we can help! We are located in the Wallace Administration Building (301) on the Phil Campbell campus and the Victor P. Poole Administration Building (100) on the Shoals campus. Advising Registration Catalog Financial Aid Testing Center Schedule a Tour Applying to NW-SCC You can submit applications for admission electronically via MyNW-SCC. Applicants must submit an unexpired, government-issued photo ID prior to the end of their first semester. Documents can be presented in person or mailed (a copy of the applicant\u2019s unexpired, government-issued photo ID must be included), or you can submit a legible copy electronically. For additional information, see the program descriptions in our Catalog and\/or contact the specific program director\/chairperson. We look forward to helping you throughout your educational journey! Apply Now Your Admission Checklist Apply for Admission Apply for Financial Aid Take the Placement test (if necessary) Forward all transcripts to the Admissions office See the Advising Center Start classes! If a student wishes to allow NW-SCC to release records to parents, grandparents, spouse, guardians, or other specified individuals, they must complete and submit the Release of Personal Information form. International Students International students should complete the Printable Admission Application and deliver it to the Admissions Office on the Shoals Campus. Admissions Policies Northwest-Shoals Community College has an open-door admissions policy for all U.S. Citizens and eligible Non-Citizens. We provide higher education for individuals who meet minimum admission requirements as set forth by the policies of the Alabama Community College system. The College does not discriminate on the basis of race, gender, color, age, sex, disability or national origin. However, the College reserves the right to individually review an applicant\u2019s admission application based on whether that admission would be prejudicial to the general welfare of the College. Acceptance or admission to the College does not mean acceptance to certain health education programs such as Nursing, Practical Nursing, Emergency Medical Services, Medical Assisting Technology, Radiology and Sonography. These programs may have additional standards for admission and progression. Any and all elements of admission requirements are subject to change without prior notice. For more information, please see the college catalog.
