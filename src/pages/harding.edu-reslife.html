New Student Assignment and Move-In Information (Fall 2022) Updates for roommates, suitemates and residence hall choices will be accepted until mid-July. In order to assign roommates or suitemates, all students wanting to be placed together must email housing@harding.edu communicating their desire to room or suite together. Residence Hall assignments will be posted on Pipeline in early August. Students will receive an email to their Harding email account including a link to access Pipeline. To access Pipeline, students will need their username and password. The DormNet Help Desk can provide assistance. Residence Hall assignments for new students are prioritized according to the following: New students are placed in order by the received date of their housing form. If two students desire to be roommates, they are placed in order by the latter of the two housing form acceptance dates. After students are placed in order as indicated above, assignments start with the earliest housing form acceptance date. Efforts will be made to meet requests for both roommate and residence hall. If both requests are not possible, priority is given to which the student placed as more important. Privileged Housing When students meet application requirements below, they may choose to apply for privileged housing which includes some Legacy Park Apartments (for women) and Cone Hall (for men). No student who is currently on probation may apply. Anyone previously approved who later incurs probation will have their approval status retracted. Residents of privileged housing must reapply each spring for the fall semester. Students may apply online. To apply, students will need to log on to their Pipeline account, click on the Student tab, then select Residence Life (Housing) and click on the Privileged Housing\/Status link. A student must have already lived in one of the regular residence halls to apply. A transfer student may apply at midterm of his or her first semester as long as he or she meets the qualifications listed below. To apply for Legacy Park (women) or Cone Hall (men), a student must: Be at least 20 years of age and meet one of the following criteria: Have completed approximately 70 credit hours Be a third-year student with 50-59 credit hours completed Have completed 27 credit hours and be 21 years of age at the time of check-in. When reviewing applications, Student Life deans consider the following: Recommendation of RLC (considers health and safety inspection record, regard for University property, compliance with curfew and other housing regulations) Cumulative GPA of at least 2.0 No probation of any kind (chapel, disciplinary, academic) Student records (Bible\/chapel attendance and Code of Conduct) When making assignments for privileged housing in Legacy Park and Cone Hall, preference will be given to seniors and senior pairs who have already filled out the privileged housing application and have been approved.
