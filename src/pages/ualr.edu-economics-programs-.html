Economics courses develop students\u2019 logical thinking skills, understanding of applied statistical analysis, and communication abilities. Many Economics majors decide to double major in Finance or in non-business majors such as philosophy, history, political science, or mathematics. Economics is a particular good choice for students planning on law school: on average, economics majors and philosophy majors do better than any other popular major on the LSAT Admissions test.
