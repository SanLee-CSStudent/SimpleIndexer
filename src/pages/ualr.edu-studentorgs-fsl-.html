Pan-Hellenic Council Forms and Resources FAQ Fraternity & Sorority Life Joining a fraternity or sorority at UA Little Rock is a unique opportunity to find friends for life and grow inside and outside of the classroom. Our fraternities and sororities are proud to maintain academic scholarship, partner in community service, and promote brotherhood\/sisterhood. UA Little Rock has three councils that govern Greek-letter organizations: College Panhellenic Council (CPC), Interfraternity Council (IFC), and National Pan-Hellenic Council (NPHC). CPC sororities (women): Chi Omega and Kappa Delta IFC fraternities (men): Delta Chi and Kappa Sigma NPHC fraternities and sororities (men and women): Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Inc., Kappa Alpha Psi Fraternity, Inc., Omega Psi Phi Fraternity, Inc., Phi Beta Sigma Fraternity, Inc., Alpha Kappa Alpha Sorority, Inc., Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Inc., and Zeta Phi Beta Sorority, Inc. Quick Facts About UA Little Rock Fraternities and Sororities 48% of members are involved in another student organization. Many hold leadership roles in the Student Government Association, Diversity Initiatives, Chancellor\u2019s Leadership Corps, athletics, and more. Organizations that benefit from UA Little Rock fraternity and sorority community service include Make-A-Wish Foundation, Girl Scouts of the USA, OUR House, Heifer International, Children\u2019s International, Habitat for Humanity, Fallen Military Heroes Campaign, AETN, Little Rock School District, Big Brothers Big Sisters of Central Arkansas, March of Dimes, Arkansas Food Bank, and many more.
