Oakwood University continues to daily monitor the surge in COVID cases. The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) identified the risk of a fast rise in Omicron variant infections\u202Fon December 20, 2021 (CDC-Omicron Variant).\u202F\u202FGiven the recent rise in COVID-19 cases associated with the Omicron variant, which appears to be more readily transmissible than the Delta variant, and the obstacles posed by cancelled flights, the University amended its\u202FSpring\u202FSemester 2022 reopening plan. \u202FPlease see below.\u202F \u202F\u202F \u202F If you are an UNDERGRADUATE student RESIDING ON CAMPUS including West Oaks and Huntsville Place, please observe the following:\u202F Undergraduate and LEAP Spring 2022 instruction began virtually on January 5 and will last through January 17, 2022, utilizing Zoom and D2L platforms.\u202FThese classes will\u202Fmeet\u202Fat the scheduled times.\u202F\u202F Log into D2L to access your courses. OUHS will preclear students whose names have been turned in by Deans and provide their Re-Entry information at the Front Gate so that they can move into their residence halls as soon as possible. Only the following tests are acceptable: PCR tests, viral RNA tests, nucleic acid tests and Antigen tests.
