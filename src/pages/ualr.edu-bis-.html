According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment in information technology occupations is projected to growth 13 percent between 2016 and 2026, \u201Cfaster than the average for all occupations.\u201D Businesses need employees who can . . . \u2026effectively use computer software and use technology to meet organizational objectives. \u2026speak the language of business and translate needs into technological solutions. \u2026make effective and efficient decisions using data. Arkansas statewide IT employment projections through 2026 are very positive. The computing systems industry is one of the top 20 growing industries in Arkansas, projected to growth by 22.11 percent from 2016-2026. The demand for business analytics professionals is 50 to 60 percent greater than the U.S. can supply (McKinsey Global Institute); Arkansas is outpacing U.S. growth in most data analytics and computing-related occupations. Wages in the state mirror those of the national average IT salaries: $62,820 for bachelor\u2019s degree, $72,517 for master\u2019s degree.
