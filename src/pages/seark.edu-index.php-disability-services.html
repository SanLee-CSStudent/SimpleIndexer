basis of race, color, sex, religion, national origin, age or disability in making decisions regarding employment, student admission or other functions, operations or activities. The College is in compliance with Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973 and the Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990 (ADA). Disability Services ensures students with disabilities have equal access to the College's academic programs, services and activities through the provision of reasonable accommodations as outlined Under the American With Disabilities Act Amendments Act (ADAAA) of 2008 Rehabilitation Act of 1973, Section 504. Who Is Eligible for Disability Services? Students with a documented physical or mental impairment that substantially limits one or more major life activities are eligible. To qualify, students must provide clear and specific evidence that a qualified professional has established a formal diagnosis of a disability. Eligible Services Eligible services will depend upon the nature of the disability and are determined on a case-by-case basis by the campus disabilities services counselors. Accommodations include but are not limited to: Sign Language Interpreters Classroom Accommodations Visual Enhancement software Contact Please contact our disability counselor and be prepared to provide them with documentation provided by a qualified professional.
