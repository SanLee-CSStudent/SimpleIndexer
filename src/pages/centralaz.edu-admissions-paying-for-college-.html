Contact Us Below Email: Student.Accounts@Centralaz.edu Phone: 520-494-5240 Expecting a Stipend or Refund? Stipends and Refunds Stipend vs. Refund \u2013 What\u2019s the difference? A stipend is excess money from a financial aid source that has approved the release of these excess funds to be disbursed directly to a student. The stipend is money left over after all tuition and fees have been paid on the student account. A stipend may result from sources that are used to pay fees such as Pell, scholarships, student loans etc. A refund is excess money that is refunded back to the student after the student self paid for fees on the student account. Money may also be refunded to a financial aid source that either over paid or is no longer providing support for the student. Expecting a Stipend or Refund? Financial aid disbursements begin taking place generally two weeks after the semester begins. A disbursement is the transfer of approved financial aid which moves from an approved status and gets placed on the student account. The amount of financial aid that is approved will cover any tuition and fees that are currently on the student account. Any money that is left over after paying all fees will be the amount that is stipend or refunded. Important: When looking at the student account in the student portal if there are parenthesis around the amount due (258.00), this means there is a credit on the student account. The Student Account Team will review all credits and determine if the money should be stipend or refunded to the student. Stipends and refunds are generally processed 7-10 business days after financial aid is applied to the student account. Stipends and refunds are sent directly to BankMobile Disbursements and processed based on the refund selection chosen by the student. To view our third-party servicer contract for disbursement management, click the information button below Third Party Third Party Agency What is a Third Party Agency? Third party agencies are businesses that the student has partnered with to pay some or all of their tuition and fees for a specific semester. Agencies can be but are not limited to the student\u2019s employer (tuition reimbursement), local or state agencies such as the Department of Economic Security, WIOA, VA, and many others. Agency Responsibility When an agency chooses to cover fees incurred they should partner with the student to ensure any and all documentation is current and correct. The agency is responsible for submitting an authorization letter to Student Accounts prior to the semester starting. Letters received after courses start may be delay invoice processing. Once an invoice is created and sent to the agency, they will be responsible for the total amount due. Failure to pay in a timely manner may hold up the students ability to register or receive transcripts for future semesters. Letters of Authorization should include the following: Student first and last name Student CAC ID number Semester Amount committing to pay including any course fees Conditions of authorization (ex: apply Pell prior to invoicing) Agency contact and mailing address to send refunds Note: Letters should be sent at the beginning of each semester. Invoices are only provided one semester at a time. Please send or email to the contact information below Student Responsibility Students are responsible for supplying the agency with documentation to support the payment process. Items may include but are not limited to registration bills, current cost of attendance including Out of State or WUE charges, course fees, and book pricing. The student should submit their documentation as soon as they can to their agency. Failure to provide current and proper documentation may delay the invoicing process. It is important for the student to read and understand the terms and conditions set by the agency. Terms and conditions may change at any time. Please contact the agency if you have questions regarding the authorization to pay. Invoices Invoices are generally created and sent to the agency by individual student 4-6 weeks after the semester begins. Students will continue to see a balance due on their student account until the agency submits payment. Invoices are sent to the agency by email. All invoices are due at the time of receipt. Agencies may choose to pay by individual student or they may send one payment for groups of students the agency is sponsoring. Overpayment & Excess Money Excess money may be left over on a student\u2019s account after the agency payment is posted. Student Accounts will review each account and determine how to handle the excess funds. The authorization letter should stipulate how to handle each scenario. Student Accounts will contact the agency to determine if the funds should be refunded or if the student may receive a stipend. The college does not determine what happens with the funds. Students should follow up with the agency if they feel the money should be given to them in a stipend. The agency must be clear on how to handle excess funds. Be sure your application is complete and accurate. Incomplete or incorrect information may delay your application and your financial aid. Students and Parents can use the IRS data retrieval tool to ensure accuracy when completing the FAFSA. The IRS data retrieval tool allows students and parents to import the IRS income information directly to the FAFSA application. The Central Arizona College Title IV School Code is 007283. Students must apply each academic year. After filing your FAFSA a Student Aid Report (SAR) will be made available for you to review for accuracy and retain for your records. The SAR contains the EFC figure. We will send you an e-mail as receipt acknowledgement indicating successful download of your FAFSA data and will inform you to check your online student account for additional information Step by step on completing the 2022-23 FAFSA online Or Step by Step on completing the 2022-23 FAFSA via mobile app Verification Applications will be automatically selected for a federally required verification process. Verification is the process of comparing the data on the student\u2019s FAFSA and actual documentation. CAC is required by federal law, to collect the necessary documentation and verify certain data elements for any discrepancies. If selected for Verification you and your family may be required to present financial documents in order for the financial aid office to verify for discrepancies and complete packaging. Verificaiton documentation will include but not limited to: Adjusted Gross Income (student and parent, if the student is dependent) Taxes Paid (student and parent, if the student is dependent) Income Earned from Work (for non-tax filers) Certain Untaxed Income Items (student and parent, if the student is dependent) Household Size Number in College (excluding parents for a dependent student) Child Support Paid Educational Purpose Any other inconsistent or conflicting information After receiving your verification documentation the process can take up to 2 weeks. If additional information is required we will contact you via phone, email and\/or mail. If updates\/corrections are made they are transmitted to CPS for processing. Upon receipt of corrections, processor ensures verification is complete. Upon completion of your financial aid file we will determine your cost of attendance and package your Federal Pell grant award. Campus based grants will be awarded accordingly based on FAFSA completion date. Federal Loans are packaged with a borrower initiated process beginning on the Federal Direct Loan Programs page. All federal grant awards are packaged anticipating full time enrollment and will be pro rated accordingly after the drop\/add period each semester. After the financial aid office completes the packaging award information is provided via student portal which gives you the types and amounts of aid you qualified for. The award terms, conditions, requirements and messages are also available. Unique Conditions Dependency Override-When applying for federal student aid, your answers to questions on Step 3 of the FAFSA classify you as either an Independent or Dependent student. Independent students report their own income and assets (and those of a spouse, if married). Dependent students report both their own and their parents\u2019 income and assets. If a student is unable to provide parent information due to extenuating circumstances, the student may complete and submit a petition to be considered an independent student. Dependency Overrides are granted on a case-by-case basis and must be documented each award year. Please contact us for more information. Special Circumstances-According to Federal regulations, a family\u2019s past income is used to assess financial need for the upcoming academic year. If a family\u2019s current income is lower due to special circumstances, a Financial Aid Administrator may be able to use the current income to assess financial need. The CAC Financial Aid Office will be reviewing such requests beginning July 1 prior to the next Academic year start. If the information provided results in a change in the type of award(s) a student can receive, their award(s) will be adjusted to reflect that change. If the review does not result in a change, the Financial Aid office will use the income information provided. Please contact us for more information. Eligibility and Return to Title IV (R2T4) Eligibility and Return to Title IV (R2T4) Eligibility To be eligible for federal financial aid, the student must: ，have financial need, (except for some loan programs) ，have a high school diploma, General Education Development (GED) Certificate ，be admitted as a \u201Cregular\u201D student according to the admission requirements listed in the college catalog ，be a U.S. citizen or eligible non-citizen ，have a valid Social Security number ，file your income tax return with the IRS, if required ，maintain satisfactory academic progress ，sign a statement of educational purpose and certification of statement on overpayment and default (on the Free Application for Federal Student Aid, FAFSA) ，register with the Selective Service, if required SAP The Federal regulations require that financial aid students maintain Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP) toward an eligible degree or certificate program. This policy applies to student applying for Title IV financial aid, including Federal Pell Grant, Federal Supplemental Educational Opportunity Grant (FSEOG), Federal Work-Study (FWS), Arizona Leveraging Educational Assistance Program (AzLEAP), and Federal Direct Stafford Loans (Stafford Subsidized, Stafford Unsubsidized, and Parent PLUS). SAP is reviewed at the end of each semester to determine financial aid eligibility for the upcoming semester. SAP is evaluated on students\u2019 previous enrollment, regardless of whether or not they received financial aid. A student\u2019s entire academic history at CAC is considered when evaluating SAP standing even when financial aid was not received. Even if students have never received federal financial aid, they may be placed on probation or suspension based on low cumulative GPA and\/or exceeding the 150% rule and\/or a low credit completion rate during their most recent semester at CAC. If a student is not making satisfactory progress, the student may appeal that decision based on mitigating circumstances. Procedures and forms for the appeal process and reinstatement of federal financial aid are available here. The Satisfactory Academic Progress Policy is also available to students in the college catalog.
