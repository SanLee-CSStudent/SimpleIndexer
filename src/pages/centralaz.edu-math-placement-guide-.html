College level math (MAT141, MAT151, MAT162) is recommended for students that meet any one of the following: 1) กร 22 on ACT Math Section 2) กร 530 on SAT Math Section* 3) กร 165 on GED Mathematical Reasoning 4) กร 237 on Accuplacer Advanced Algebra & Functions 5) กร 286 on Accuplacer Quantitative Reasoning, Algebra & Statistics (eligible to enroll in MAT141 only) 6) Students with AP Calculus AB or BC Score of 2 are eligible to enroll into MAT221 7) Students with AP Statistics Score of 2 are eligible to enroll in MAT162 8) Appropriate placement via Skills Assessment Tool Note Students with Accuplacer scores may be eligible to \u201Cbump up\u201D their placement according to attached Multiple Measures Sheet. Due to COVID19 some students may not be able to complete the Accuplacer. For students who have not completed Accuplacer or other placement options listed above, college level math may be appropriate if one of the following is met; please discuss your pathway and placement with your advisor using the attached reference guides: 1) Proficient or Highly Proficient on AZ Merit Algebra II 2) กร 2.8 cumulative high school G.P.A and กร 3.0 high school math G.P.A. *G.P.A. within last 3 years If the student and advisor determine college level math is not appropriate then MAT087 is suggested for non-STEM students. MAT097 is suggested for STEM and Business students. Please reference attached guides to assist with placement. *New SAT which began spring 2016 **Students with AP, CLEP, IB, or Cambridge Scores can see the statewide \u201CExam Equivalency Guide\u201D under \u201CTools\u201D at www.aztransfer.com. Want to brush up before your math class begins? Please go to the tab \u201CFREE math boot camps\u201D for additional information. If you have questions about a math course, please feel free to contact the Mathematics Division Chair, Beverly Demaline, at 480-677-7834 or beverly.demaline@centralaz.edu.
