Finances & Scholarships Review Your Scholarship Eligibility Freshman Academic Scholarships are awarded based on high school work, test scores, and residency. View more information about these scholarships, upgrade opportunities, and important deadlines at uah.edu\/scholarships. Apply for Financial Aid To be considered for all types of federal aid, U.S. citizens and permanent residents should submit the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) at fafsa.gov using the UAH School Code (001055). Students planning to utilize VA educations benefits should contact the Military and Veterans Programs Office at va@uah.edu or 256.8243.2758. Accept Your Scholarship and\/or Financial Aid Accept or decline your scholarship and\/or financial aid awards through your my.uah.edu account once your receive your official financial aid award package information from the Office of Financial Aid. This information will be sent to your official @uah.edu email account. For step by step instructions, visit the Accept My Scholarship page. U.S. citizens and permanent residents who have not already applied for financial aid should submit the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) to be considered for federal aid using the UAH school code, 001055. Student Services & Housing Apply for Housing All first-time, full-time freshman students with a permanent legal residence beyond a 30 mile radius from campus are required to live in campus residence halls their first year. Apply for housing with Housing & Residence Life. Select a Meal Plan Students are automatically assigned a meal plan based on if they plan to live on-campus or off-campus. If you prefer the default meal plan, you do not have to submit a request or select a plan. Requests are only needed from students who prefer another meal plan option or an upgrade. All requests must be submitted to the Charger Card Office no later than the last day of the 2nd week of classes each semester. All full-time, undergraduate students are required to have a meal plan. Learn about available options by visiting Dining Services. Submit Your Immunization Records Submit your immunization records using the Immunization Form and Tuberculosis Risk Questionnaire. For information or assistance, visit the Student Health Center, call 256.824.6978, or email immunizations@uah.edu. Apply for Student Disability Services If you have a disability that requires academic or physical accommodations, contact Disability Support Services at 256.824.6203 to file an application and set up a meeting to determine the appropriate accommodations. Access Your Online Services Access myUAH To set up your myUAH account, UAH\u2019s secure web portal, visit reset.uah.edu and follow the prompts to create your password. As a UAH student, you will use your myUAH account by visiting my.uah.edu to access campus information, accept\/decline financial aid, register for classes, view your grades, see your academic transcript, and pay your bill. Questions about the differences between your Charger Status and myUAH accounts? Charger Status vs myUAH If an emergency arises that impacts you as an individual, the university will refer to your emergency contact information. Verify Your Information Check that your contact information, major, and application term are correct on your admission letter. If changes are needed, contact the Office of Admissions at 256.824.2773 or admissions@uah.edu. Academics Submit Your Final Transcript A final high school transcript, including date of graduation, is due within four weeks of graduation. If the final official transcript is not received, you may become ineligible to register for courses or your schedule may be adjusted. You can send your official transcripts to UAH using Docufide\/Parchment, E-Scrip, or Student Clearinghouse, or ask your school to send your official hard-copy transcript to UAH Admissions, 301 Sparkman Drive, SSB, Suite 106, Huntsville, AL 35899. Submit Dual Enrollment\/AP\/IB\/CLEP Credit To receive credit for dual-enrollment courses, you will need to submit an official college transcript. To receive credit for Advanced Placement (AP), International Baccalaureate (IB), or College Level Examination Program (CLEP) exams, official test scores must be sent by the testing agency. Visit CollegeBoard and use UAH school code 1854 for AP and CLEP, and visit IBO for IB. Once official transcripts and test scores have been received and evaluated, you may view your credit on your Academic Transcript through your my.uah.edu account. Determine Your Academic Placement Academic placement for new students is determined by your ACT\/SAT test results, AP scores, and\/or previous college credit. Determine your academic placement by reviewing our Academic Placement Guidelines. Students who would like to test for a higher course placement in math, foreign languages, and English should do so prior to attending Freshman Orientation. Academics Submit Your Final Transcript If you are currently enrolled at another institution, you must submit a final copy of your transcript to the Office of Admissions as soon as possible to ensure timely evaluation of transfer credit. If the final official transcript is not received, you may become ineligible to register for courses or your schedule may be adjusted. Upon admission, transcript evaluations will be completed as early as possible, but no later than the first semester of enrollment. You will receive notification via the email account you provide on your application for admission, as well as your official UAH email account, when the evaluation is complete. Once your transfer credit evaluation is complete, you may view your credit on your Academic Transcript through your my.uah.edu account.
