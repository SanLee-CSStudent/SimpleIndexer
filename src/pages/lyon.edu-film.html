You can go behind the scenes with the film and media studies minor. You will learn about film history, criticism, analysis, theory, and the techniques that put the human experience on the screen. Through this minor, you will see how our cultures and disciplines are understood and propagated through visual media. You will become more aware of film as an art form, as a technology, and as a means of political and cultural expression. % of Lyon graduates are employed or in grad school within 6 months of graduation :1 student-to-teacher ratio Meet the Faculty Details Courses Careers Admissions Why Film and Media at Lyon? At Lyon College, following the liberal arts tradition, students learn to connect the medium of film with other disciplines. Students are thus encouraged to combine the film minor with different majors. Pair your passion with an interdisciplinary education! Lyon provides opportunities to engage with film and audio-visual media through organizations like the Arkansas Cinema Society, as well as through KILT, Lyon College\u2019s radio station. Faculty Members Dr. James Martell, Dr. Helen Robbins, Dr. Awah Sidjeck, Dr. Vida Owusu-Bouateng, Professor Ian Campbell Ways To Get Involved The Lyon Film Cycle shows multiple European films each semester. Academic presentations and discussions follow the film. Lyon College\u2019s radio station, KILT, develops both audio and audio-visual shows where students get to practice and reflect on the visual medium as a platform for both entertainment and education.
