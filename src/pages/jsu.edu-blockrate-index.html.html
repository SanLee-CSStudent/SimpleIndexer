Block Rate\/Flat Rate FAQs What is \u201Cblock rate\u201D or \u201Cflat rate\u201D tuition? Block rate or flat rate tuition is defined as charging a single tuition rate for credits within a defined range (e.g., 12-18 semester hours). What are the terms of block rate tuition? Undergraduate students are charged the same set tuition rate for 12-18 credit hours in the fall and spring semesters, plus fees. The amount of the block tuition rate is subject to change annually. Why is JSU moving to block rate tuition? The block rate tuition at JSU is part of an overall effort to enhance student success, persistence and create an easier and more affordable path to graduation in four years. This rate provides students with an incentive to take more hours in support of that goal. Students require 15 hours a semester or 30 hours a year to stay on track to graduate in four years. Research indicates significant benefits for students who take 15 hours per semester: better academic performance, higher retention rates and increased graduation rates. Additionally, the block rate is a way to help students reduce the overall cost of their education. When will block rate tuition take effect? The 2022 Block Rate Tuition will become effective with at the beginning of the Fall semester. Is block rate tuition a new concept? No. Several universities in Alabama have established block rate tuition. Evidence nationwide suggests that block rate tuition is a promising strategy for increasing student success and degree-completion in 4-years. Do I still have to pay fees with the block rate tuition? Yes. Students will still pay the General University Fee as well as distance learning fees, program fees, and applicable course fees. Does this include Dual Enrollment Students? No, Dual Enrollment Students are not included in Block Rate Tuition. How will block rate tuition affect students taking more or fewer than 12-18 semester hours? Undergraduates taking fewer or more than 12-18 hours will default to the per-credit-hour tuition plan rather than the block rate plan. Will block rate tuition be available to graduate students? No. Block rate tuition applies to undergraduates only and has been established to help students earn their BA\/BS degree in four years. What are the benefits of a student taking 15 semester hours or more? To graduate within four years, undergraduates typically need to take 15 hours per semester. In addition to saving time and money, research shows that students who take 15 hours per semester have higher academic performance, higher retention rates and increased graduation rates. What is the best way to ensure success for students who typically take a 12-credit course load but want to increase the number of credit hours they take each semester? Students should consult their academic advisor to determine best options. Students also have access to free assistance from the Student Success Center in the Houston Cole Library for tutoring and academic support, supplemental instruction, academic plans and success coaching. For more information, see the Student Success Center website. What if a student is not sure he\/she can manage 12 semester hours or more per semester? JSU wants all admitted students to be successful and recognizes that a student\u2019s ability to manage his\/her academic workload is dependent on a variety of personal circumstances. These include learning style, , financial and work commitments, and family dynamics, to name a few. JSU academic support specialists, financial aid counselors, career services specialists and others across campus can help guide students in making smart decisions about academic load given the student\u2019s personal circumstances. Contact the Student Success Center or your academic advisor for guidance and direction. What if a student takes 15-18 credit hours and begins to struggle with their academic workload? In consultation with their academic advisor students can withdraw from some courses before the published deadline. From a financial aid perspective, it is advisable to maintain at least 12 credit hours per semester. Students receiving financial aid should talk to a financial aid advisor if they are considering dropping below 12 credit hours due to the possible negative impact on financial aid. Does block rate tuition apply to summer courses? No, block rate tuition only applies during the fall and spring semesters.
