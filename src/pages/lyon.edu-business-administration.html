Major Requirements Minor Requirements Business Administration Courses Business Administration Major If you want to understand complex business environments, administrative practices and processes, and the structure and role of organizations, then a business administration major may be a great fit. This major will introduce you to the concepts and theories of management and decision-making while providing experience-based opportunities in which to apply these concepts. % of Business majors are employed or in grad school within 6 months of graduation Recognized as a College of Distinction in Business 1 in 3 Fortune 500 CEOs have liberal arts degrees Meet the Faculty Details Courses Careers Admissions Internships Scholarships What Makes This Program Unique Students may focus their studies and select an emphasis in one of two areas: management or finance. As students advance, they will have the opportunity to apply their knowledge in a business simulation project and later in an internship or thesis project. Students will also have the opportunity to apply their learned skills to the management of a real fund, through an endowment-funded investment portfolio. Faculty Members Dr. Mahbubul Kabir, Dr. Radek Szulga, and Dr. Angela Buchanan Ways To Get Involved on Campus In the business department, Professor in Business and Economics Dr. Angela Buchanan serves as the chapter advisor for Lyon\u2019s Enactus group, an organization that connects students with business leaders through entrepreneurial-based projects. Lyon\u2019s Enactus group recently achieved a partnership with nonprofit organization Main Street Batesville to develop a makers\u2019 space for local and regional artisans in downtown Batesville. Careers for Business Administration Majors The business administration major offers graduates the flexibility to choose between entry-level managerial positions and advanced study. It is particularly appropriate for individuals seeking advanced study in business, law, planning, and healthcare management. Starting Median U.S. Salary For a Business Administration Major The starting median salary in the U.S. for a college graduate with a Business Administration degree is $43,000.
