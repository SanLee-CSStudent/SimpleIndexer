[\/eplus-bg-color] The Edge Leadership Experience Welcoming future leaders The Edge Leadership Experience is a unique opportunity for first-year incoming students to learn and practice leadership skills. Program mission\/purpose: The purpose of The Edge Leadership Experience is to provide first-year students with a four-day camp experience that welcomes them to campus, builds upon their leadership skills, and introduces them to engagement and leadership opportunities within the University. Ultimately, The Edge Leadership Experience provides a unique opportunity for students to learn and practice leadership skills while transitioning into the college environment. Click here to register for EDGE 2022! August 22-25, 2022 Potential participant schedule: Day 1: Time commitment: 4pm\u20138pm Day 2: Time commitment: 9am\u20137pm Day 3: Time commitment: 9am\u20137pm Day 4: Time commitment: 9am\u20137pm Program goals and learning outcomes Goal #1: Deepen students\u2019 understanding of their leadership identity by exploring values, strengths, and character. Learning outcome: Students will understand and be able to articulate what makes them a leader in their respective communities. Goal #2: Assist students in discovering their identity as leaders at the collegiate level through the Five Practices of Exemplary Leadership and the Social Change Model of leadership. Learning outcome: Students will put the Five Practices of Exemplary Leadership and the Social Change Model of leadership into action on campus. Goal #3: Introduce students to opportunities for leadership engagement at Northern Arizona University. Learning outcome: Students will participate in leadership opportunities to expand their comfort levels. Goal #4: Establish a cohort of fellow student leaders with a meaningful connection at Northern Arizona University. Learning outcome: Students will develop a sense of belonging by cultivating lasting relationships and discovering their place at NAU. Additional program information Who is involved? Accordion Closed 100 first-year students from all backgrounds will be selected for the program. We encourage participation from students who were involved in student organizations and leadership positions in high school and from students who have not had previous opportunities but want to make leadership part of their college experience. Upper-division students who are current student leaders serve as facilitators for the program. What should I expect? Accordion Closed If accepted, you will be placed in a small group designed to enhance your campus connections. Small groups will participate in workshops, presentations, activities, and challenges that strengthen bonds and provide an engaging experience.
