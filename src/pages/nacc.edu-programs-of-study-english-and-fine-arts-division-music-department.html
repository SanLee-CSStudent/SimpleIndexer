Music Department Programs of Study A- A+ Health Science Division\/Nursing Mathematics Division English and Fine Arts Division Theatre Department Child Development Music Department English Department Art Department Business and Computer Science Division Natural Sciences Division Skills Training Social Sciences, Speech and Foreign Languages Division Tech Programs Athens State University at NACC Academic Maps Class Schedule Catalog Music Department Stacy Morris Instructor of Instrumental Ensembles Office: GY 240 Phone: Ext. 2307 E-mail: morriss@nacc.edu Sara Hagood Markham Instructor of Vocal Ensembles Office: GY 234 Phone: Ext. 2215 E-mail: markhams@nacc.edu Northeast Alabama Community College's music department, located in the Beck Health and Fine Arts building, offers a full range of courses in fundamentals, musicianship, theory, appreciation, as well as classes in piano, voice and guitar. Private lessons are available on most instruments, including voice. The Department also offers a wide range of performing ensembles which are both an integral part of every musician's education and an opportunity for adults from the community to participate in the performance of great masterworks. Instrumental performing ensembles at Northeast include the Jazz Ensemble, Concert Band, Mustang Stage Band and Chamber Winds (all auditioned). Vocal performing ensembles include the Chorus (auditioned mixed voices) and Encore! (auditioned show choir). NACC Performing Ensemble Scholarships Performing Ensemble Scholarships are available to any NACC student, not just Music Majors! Each FULL TUITION scholarship covers one year, and is renewable for one additional year 2022-2023 Music Ensemble Scholarship Auditions Auditions will be held in person on February 14-15th, 2022. You will be contacted with your assigned audition day and time. Contact Sara Markham for more information about Vocal Ensembles. Contact Stacy Morris for more information on Instrumental Ensembles. Vocal Ensemble Requirements Perform a memorized solo in any style, no longer than 2 minutes, that shows off your singing abilities at their best. It should be with an instrumental accompaniment track (no other voices), or with a live instrument. Sing one verse of the National Anthem without accompaniment, in a range that is comfortable for you. Instrumental Ensemble Requirements Wind Instruments: Two major scales (2 octaves preferable) and one prepared piece Percussion: On snare drum please demonstrate 3 rudiments Paradiddle- slow,fast,slow, and 2 others of your choosing, along with a prepared etude. Mallet- 3 scales of your choice and a prepared piece. String Instruments & Piano: Two pieces in contrasting styles (Classical, Rock, Bluegrass, Folk, Jazz, Hymn, etc.) The department's facilities include: A piano lab, which is open five days a week. It contains 12 Yamaha Clavinovas, each equipped with headphones for private practicing. A Music Theory lab which contains several computer stations. Each computer is equipped with Auralia (ear training software), Band In A Box (accompaniment software) and Sibelius (notation\/composition software). There is also an extensive library of CDs, DVDs, and video cassettes (along with listening\/viewing facilities) and music reference books. A 340 seat auditorium with stage, featuring a 5.8 foot Yamaha concert grand piano. Practice rooms for individual vocal and instrumental practice. Fully equipped Band and Choral rehearsal rooms. Full tuition scholarships are available by audition for individuals participating in a performing ensemble. Further information related to curriculum and participation in performing ensembles may be obtained by calling the Music Department at 256-228-6001 or 256-638-4418, extensions 2215 and 2307.
