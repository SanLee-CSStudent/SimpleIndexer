Research Focuses on University Students\u2019 Spirituality At the beginning of his administration, President Leslie Pollard outlined the case for immediately engaging in an unprecedented research study of Oakwood students\u2019 spiritual commitments and beliefs. The LifeCore? family of surveys was developed to assess the spiritual needs of the University\u2019s student body of which 852 students completed the 52-item survey in its first launch. Complete Survey LifeCore? 2011 data indicated that Oakwood students reported deeper levels of commitment than that of their peers and other born-again Christians (per Barna) in every category of commitment. Home atmosphere was correlated with a positive faith. LifeCore? 2013 was a mini survey which assessed the student body in relation to spiritual development. The 29-question survey in which 1100 students shared information regarding their prayer and bible study habits, as well as the significance of demographics, parental influence, peer influence, and personal experiences in relationship to the shaping and development of the individual\u2019s faith. LifeCore? 2014 focused on writing and publishing a paper of the 2011 and 2013 survey results. Directions DIRECTIONS: Students, let your voices be heard. Tell us about your spiritual experience. We would appreciate you filling out this quick survey. LifeCore 2015-16 is the third version of the LifeCore Survey of Student Spirituality. Your responses are voluntary and will be confidential. All responses will be compiled and analyzed as a group. Individual respondents will not be identified.
