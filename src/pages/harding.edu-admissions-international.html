Read this information in Spanish | Chinese How to Apply International students seeking admission to the University should submit the following: Step 1 \u2014 Apply: A nonrefundable $50 fee is required to apply. When applying online, you should pay online. You may also pay over the phone by calling 800-477-4407. Apply Online Step 2 \u2014 Send us your complete academic credentials: This includes certificates, diplomas, matriculation examination results, degrees, etc. These must be sent directly from your school and mailed to the following address: Harding University Undergraduate Admissions Box 12255 Searcy, AR 72149-5615 Step 3 \u2014 Send your TOEFL or IELTS scores: If official language is not English. These must be sent directly from the testing center. All international students, both undergraduate and graduate, who enroll at Harding University are expected to present a TOEFL score of 550 or higher (or an equivalent combined score of 70 on the IBT) or a 6.0 on the IELTS. Step 4 \u2014 Send a copy of your passport to admissions@harding.edu. You're Accepted! What's Next? Step 5 \u2014 Pay enrollment fee and select housing preferences (deadline is May 1): Once you have decided to join the herd, commit by paying your $250 enrollment fee and select your housing preferences. Priority Commitment (deadline is Feb 1): Students that commit early pay a discounted Enrollment Fee of $150 (typically $250), are more likely receive their dorm of choice, and will have priority registration for Bison Bound (new student orientation). *Housing preference(s) is based on a student\u2019s commitment date (payment of enrollment fee). If a student has a roommate and\/or suitemate preference(s), all other parties must have the same or earlier commitment date for all preferences to be considered. Students that have committed early cannot advance the place in line of other students that have committed later. *The enrollment fee covers all First-Year Experience costs and activities. This includes Bison Bound (student orientation), Impact (welcome week), and other FYE events such as the Freshman Retreat and the Pre-union Dinner. This fee is refundable if notified by May 1 for fall applicants, Oct. 1 for spring applicants, and April 1 for summer applicants. Step 6 \u2014 Send your immunization records to admissions@harding.edu: The Arkansas Department of Health requires proof of two MMR - Measles, Mumps, Rubella - immunizations for college students. The MMR immunization must be received on or after your first birthday. Please send your immunization records to admissions@harding.edu. Exemptions from this must be filed with the Arkansas Department of Health. Step 7 \u2014 Submit your confidential reference When all items for admission have been received, you will be considered for admission. An I-20 will be sent only after a student has been accepted to the University. Step 8 \u2014 Sign up for Bison Bound Housing Forms Apartment Preference Residence Hall Preference Cost & Financial Aid The estimated annual cost for tuition, fees, room and board is $28,978. Although international students are not required to take the ACT or SAT, the Office of Undergraduate Admissions Services strongly recommends that students take one of these tests to possibly qualify for scholarship money. Although Federal financial aid is not available, some international students may be eligible for private loans. Also, international students may be eligible for scholarship assistance up to 1\/4 tuition. However, certain restrictions do apply. Harding University cannot guarantee part-time employment during the regular semesters, nor during the summer months. In no case will a student be able to receive financial assistance from the university which will exceed actual tuition costs. Funds for room, board, books, clothing and miscellaneous expenses must come from some other source. Estimated upon enrollment in 15 credit hours. Most students attend only these two semesters; however, the summer sessions begin in June and enable some students to acquire additional credit equivalent to about two-thirds of the coursework of a regular semester. See also Campus Calendar Campus Events International Insurance The International Office is required to have insurance on all International Students. We currently have a policy with Geo-Blue (geobluestudents.com) that covers most illnesses at 100%. It does not cover eye exams or dental exams, however, it does cover both of these if there is a medical reason, such as trauma to the eyes or mouth. When you arrive, you will simply drop by the International Office and we will get you enrolled. (Our office is in the Student Center, Room 237) The charge will be sent to the Business Office, and you will be able to pay for it through your Harding account. The cost for the 2019-2020 school year is $103.45\/month. The cost for the 2020-2021 school year is $108.60\/month. If you are an athlete, you are required to be enrolled in the International Insurance coverage. If you have any questions, please feel free to contact the International Office.
