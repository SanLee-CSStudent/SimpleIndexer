Online Application Nondiscrimination Policy It is the official policy of the Alabama Department of Postsecondary Education and Northwest-Shoals Community College that no person in Alabama shall on the grounds of race, color, disability, sex, religion, creed, national origin, or age, be excluded from participation, be denied the benefits of, or be subjected to discrimination under any program, activity, or employment. The College complies with nondiscriminatory regulations under Title VI and Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964; Title IX Education Amendment of 1972; Section 504 Rehabilitation Act of 1973; and the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) of 1990. International Students International students MUST submit their application for admission by mail or in person due to additional admission requirements. Virtual Learning Guide (for new Distance Education students) Students who plan to take online courses MUST also complete the Virtual Learning Guide (orientation) prior to the first day of class. Students should receive an email following registration with instructions on how to complete the orientation. Contact April Cookson (cookson@nwscc.edu or 256-331-5395) for information about this activity. Transfer Students An applicant who has previously attended another regionally accredited college will be considered a transfer student and will be required to furnish official transcripts of all work attempted at all said institutions. Transfer students will also be required to submit the documents required of first-time college students including Selective Service documentation, if applicable. Students who are currently attending another college or university who would like to take courses at NW-SCC to transfer to their primary college should review the information on transient students. Transient Students Students who attend another college full-time and wish to earn credits at NW-SCC to transfer to their primary college may be admitted to NW-SCC as transient students. The student must submit an application for admission to NW-SCC and provide a transient letter from the primary college that certifies that the credits they earn will be accepted as a part of their academic program. The transient permission form must be properly signed by the appropriate official at the parent institution and must list the specific College course(s) for which the student has been approved to enroll. Transient students are not required to submit transcripts. See the current College Catalog Admission section for additional information.
