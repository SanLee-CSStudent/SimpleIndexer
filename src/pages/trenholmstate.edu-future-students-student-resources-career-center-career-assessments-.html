Career Center \/ Career Assessments Are you having trouble deciding your career path? The first step is to understand your interests, abilities, and values. The following free tools can assist you with that process: Myers-Briggs Personality Test The Myers-Briggs Personality Test, the MBTI test, or even \"that personality test\", they Myers-Briggs¢ç assessment traces the patterns in your behavior to one of 16 distinct personality types. It gives you framework for understanding yourself and appreciating the differences in others. Type Focus-Free Registration Students will discover which careers best suit their individual strengths through three interactive career assessments. They will narrow their search from thousands of career choices to a few dozen and become confident that they are on the right track. MyPlan MyPlan.com helps students plan more fulfilling lives by making well-informed decisions about their education and careers. Whether you're decidsing on what college to go to, choosing a major, planning ahead for your first career, or thinking about making a career change, MyPlan.com can help you explore options and bring clarity and insight into figuring out what's right for you. O*NET Interest Profiler The O*NET Interest Profiler can help you find out what your interest are and how they relate to the world of work. You can find out what you like to do. It will help you decide what kinds of careers you might want to explore. Holland Codes Discover the best jobs for you with the powerful system of Holland career codes. This free test will identify your strongest career interest among six occupational themes: Realistic, Investigative, Artistic, Social, Enterprising, and Conventional. You'll discover your top interest area and what that means for you. You'll also learn how you scored in the other 5 interest area. Finally, you'll see a list of suggested careers based on your profile.
