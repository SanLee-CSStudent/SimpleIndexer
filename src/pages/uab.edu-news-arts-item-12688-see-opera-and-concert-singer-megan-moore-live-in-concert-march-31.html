Award-winning singer Megan Moore will perform Thursday, March 31, presented by the University of Alabama at Birmingham\u2019s Alys Stephens Performing Arts Center. The Young Concert Artist series performance is free and open to the public with advance registration. Register online. The 7 p.m. performance will be in the Alys Stephens Center\u2019s Jemison Concert Hall. Call 205-975-2787 or visit AlysStephens.org. Mezzo-soprano Moore is making waves as a young artist of versatility and depth, according to her artist\u2019s statement. An award-winning opera and concert singer, she was awarded First Prize in the 2020 Young Concert Artists International Auditions and was a top prize winner at Gerda Lissner Lieder\/Song Competition in 2019. For her appearance as Dorabella in \u201CCos? fan tutte\u201D at The Juilliard School, OperaWire praised her \u201Cstrength as an actress\u201D and \u201Cundeniable sex appeal.\u201D In 2021, Moore had major debuts: As the sorceress Medea in Handel\u2019s \u201CTeseo,\u201D a tour-de-force role that showcased her trademark vocal agility, at the Juilliard School, then as a member of the prestigious Santa Fe Opera Apprentice Program for Singers, where she sang the role of Ino in the world premiere of \u201CThe Lord of Cries\u201D by John Corigliano and librettist Mark Adamo. On the concert stage, Moore\u2019s art song repertoire spans the genre, from German Lieder and French M?lodie to Scandinavian song and new music by living American composers.
