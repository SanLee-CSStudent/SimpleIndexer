Our concentration-based curriculum offers twelve professional pathways include Core, Professional or Public Accounting, Business Economics, Corporate Finance and Investments, Consumer and Digital Marketing, Management, Human Resources, International Management, and Small Enterprise Management. Looking for technical education? We offer certificate and associates programs in Automotive Technology, Computer Graphic Technology, Electrical Engineering Technology, and Welding Technology. Students wishing to complete a four-year degree can choose the Bachelor of Animation Technology, Bachelor of Electrical Engineering, Bachelor of Science in Organizational Leadership, or Bachelor of Applied Science in Logistics Operations. She is a professor of economics and holds the Joel R. Stubblefield Endowed Business Chair. Prior to her role as associate dean, she was the department head for Accounting, Economics, and Finance. Settlage has earned multiple awards for her service as a student organization faculty advisor at UAFS and received the 2013 Lucille Speakman Master Teacher award in recognition of teaching excellence. She is a recognized community expert on the topic of economic impact and a champion for personal financial literacy. Her published research addresses topics ranging from bank failure and government-sponsored lending programs to student learning.
