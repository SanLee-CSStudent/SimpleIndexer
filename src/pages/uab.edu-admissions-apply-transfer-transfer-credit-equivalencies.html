Two-year public college in Alabama It\u2019s called the Statewide Transfer and Articulation Reporting System (STARS), but what it really means is transferring your credits from the two-year college you\u2019ve been attending to the four-year college you want to attend. (That\u2019s UAB, by the way.) The eligibility of your transfer credits will depend on the subject matter of each course and the accreditation of the college that awarded the credit. Remedial, technical, and vocational credit usually isn\u2019t considered. If you\u2019re transferring from an accredited college, you\u2019ll be able to transfer up to half the required credit hours toward your degree from UAB. The total number of hours will appear on your UAB transcript\u2014but the academic department offering your major will be the one to determine if each course will apply to your degree. The easiest way to see which of your credits will transfer is to check the STARS Web database. You may be closer to a UAB degree than you think. STARS Web Database Other colleges or universities The eligibility of your transfer credits will depend on the subject matter of each course and the accreditation of the college that awarded the credit. If your college is accredited by one of the regional agencies recognized by the American Council on Education, then your credits probably will be eligible for transfer. The total number of transferred hours will appear on your UAB transcript\u2014but the academic department offering your major will determine if each course applies to your degree requirements. If you\u2019re transferring from an accredited two-year college, then you\u2019ll be able to transfer up to half the required credit hours toward your UAB degree. If you\u2019re transferring from an accredited four-year institution, then you\u2019ll be able to transfer an unlimited number of credits. However, remedial, technical, vocational, and orientation credits usually aren\u2019t considered. Want a preview? International institution Students who have completed college-level work in a country other than the United States are eligible to receive transfer credit if the institution is officially recognized by the respective Ministry of Education or governing educational body.
