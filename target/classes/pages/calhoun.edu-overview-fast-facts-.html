Who We Are: With approximately 10,000 credit students, Calhoun remains Alabama\u2019s largest two-year college and the 6th largest higher education institution (two-year & four-year) in the state (behind the University of Alabama, Auburn University, the University of Alabama \u2013 Birmingham, Troy State, & the University of South Alabama). Calhoun has been recognized as one of the nation\u2019s \u201CTop 150 Community Colleges\u201D by the Aspen Institute. Calhoun\u2019s Huntsville\/Cummings Research Park campus is the only two-year college campus in the nation located in a major research park. Calhoun is a member of the Achieving the Dream National Reform Network, which provides resources, support and evidence-based strategies focused on closing achievement gaps and increasing student retention, persistence and completion rates. The top five institutions to which Calhoun students transfer are: University of Alabama in Huntsville \u2013 25% Athens State University \u2013 24% Auburn University \u2013 12% University of Alabama \u2013 11% University of Alabama \u2013 Birmingham \u2013 8% Calhoun\u2019s top three award-producing degree programs are: General Studies Advanced Manufacturing Registered Nursing The College is proud of its quality programs. Currently, 11 degree programs are nationally accredited. ACHE (Alabama Commission on Higher Education) Fall 2016 data indicates that Calhoun remains the \u201Ccollege of choice\u201D for students from this region. Of the students from this area who attend public college, 29% of those from Madison County, 45% of those from Limestone County, 42% of those from Morgan County and 31% of those from Lawrence County chose to attend Calhoun . Calhoun is the region\u2019s community college: \u201CCalhoun demonstrates that economic development needs to be a regional affair \u2013 Calhoun gives us a tremendous advantage.\u201D \u2013 Former Congressman Bud Cramer Funded through a $3.47M U.S. Department of Labor grant, the Alabama Center for Excellence in Clean Energy Technology houses the College\u2019s Renewable Energy associate\u2019s degree program, the state\u2019s first and only such program. The building is the state\u2019s first Platinum-level LEED certified facility under the new system.
